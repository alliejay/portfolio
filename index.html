ABOUT ALLIE

Albert Schweitzer's quote regarding success and happiness embodies the ideals which I believe whole-heartedly, and which have changed my life for the better in this past year.

I graduated from the University of Massachusetts, Amherst in 2012 and shortly thereafter started a career in the Cell Tower Industry. While I enjoyed my role as a Lease Analyst, and I had the utmost respect for my managers & co-workers, I (like most) am not overwhelmingly interested in steel structures. I found myself envying those who went to work every day truly loving what they did.

It quickly became apparent that web development & design brings out a side of me I hadn't seen in years. I get excited to go to school every day and learn more about the amazing things I will become capable of as a front end engineer.

"Beginner Blogger Thoughts"

Saturday, January 9, 2016 | 5 PM

How do you start your first blog post? What should the topic be? There are so many options. Even before thinking about that, what blogging platform should you use? There are tons of options. These are the questions that have been confronting me recently as my time at The Iron Yard was winding down & I now find myself, for the first time in months, with a Saturday and no big project looming overhead.

It's a bit weird to be done at TIY. Well "done" may not be all that accurate. I already know myself and others will be right back on campus this next week, heckling our Campus Director for insight on everythig job related and learning/re-learning all sorts of things. TIY's whole "we teach you how to learn" thing is definitely true. All I want to do is expand my knowledge and TIY has really helped me figure out the best ways to do so. So what am I learning right now? About blogging platforms/static site generators. One specifically.

I've wanted to start a blog for quite a while, but with the hours I was putting in at TIY, I never seemed to find time. Just deciding how you're going to build/manage your blog takes a lot of time, never mind actually doing it. But I still want to get started. This is why my first few posts will be old school html right on the page I'm working on. Caveman status, baby. Once I have my new blogging platform figured out, I'll refactor things. You might be curious which platform I chose (or not, I don't care) - well, I picked Metalsmith.io.

The cool thing about Metalsmith is Metalsmith at its core: everything is a plugin. Everything. It is completely configureable to your exact needs. This level of customization excites me more than eating another brownie sunday from Rutledge Cab Co. (oh, so good). There also appears to be a great community around the platform, they even have their own slack channel which I thought was pretty cool. Metalsmith is a Node.js tool, which is also great for me because since I love (& just spent 12K learning) JavaScript, anything that furthers my knowledge in that department is excellent.

So that's basically where I stand. Over the next couple of weeks my main goals will be tinkering with Metalsmith and bugging my Campus Director (just kidding, she loves us. We think.) to look at another, and another, cover letter. Hopefully I'll find time to continiously update here with learning moments & anything else that lights up my world.
